## Week 2: The Humbling ğŸ˜¤

Turns out there's this thing called "color theory" and apparently you can't just slap random colors together and expect it to work?? Who knew??? (Everyone. Everyone knew.)

### Things I Learned the Hard Way:

1. **Dithering is NOT optional** - It's the difference between "cool retro sprite" and "my eyes hurt"
2. **Outlines matter** - Black outlines on everything = rookie move
3. **Color palettes exist for a reason** - No, you can't use all 16 million colors
4. **Animation is HARD** - Making 4 frames took me 6 hours (not a flex)

## Week 4-8: The Grind ğŸ’ª

This is where it got real. I joined pixel art Discord servers (shoutout to all the patient souls who answered my dumb questions ğŸ™).

### What Actually Helped:

**1. Daily Challenges**  
Even if it's just one tiny sprite, make SOMETHING every day. Consistency > Perfection

**2. Study the Masters**  
Look at old SNES games. Like actually LOOK. Zoom in. See how they solved problems with limited colors.

**3. Steal Like an Artist**  
(Legally) Use references. No one expects you to invent perspective from scratch.

**4. Embrace the Suck**  
Your first 100 sprites will be mid. That's NORMAL. Keep going.
